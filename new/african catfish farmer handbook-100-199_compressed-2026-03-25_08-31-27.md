In most cases unsuccessful hatching has multiple causes from a variety of angles and therefore not easy to spot and to correct, but I try to list the issues which usually go wrong:

Brood stocks are of inferior quality because they have not been given good feed and/or have been underfed and/or are less than 12 months old.

## Suspended solids

Suspended solids are particles in the water which make the water cloudy. This turbidity can not only originate from waste materials, such as sand, laterite and clay, but also from the

faeces produced by the fish or from an algal bloom. Catfish are not very sensitive to turbid

water and thrive well in it. During the rainy season especially, the water can be very turbid because of soil erosion, but the fish will not face many problems. However, in the rainy season I have noticed greater mortality when using river water than during the dry season. I believe it isn't the solids suspended in the water that cause this problem, but the runoff

from farms, containing pesticides and herbicides. Of course, in the hatchery you want to

have clear water and when you use borehole water this will be usually the case. In general, high levels of suspended solids in the water will cause the oxygen level of the water to be low, because of a high biological oxygen demand (BOD). If the catfish have developed their extra breathing organ, they just compensate for this by taking oxygen from the air.
