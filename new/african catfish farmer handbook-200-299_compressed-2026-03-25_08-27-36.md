## Definition of a virus

A virus is a small particle that is capable of infecting a cell and has the potential to cause

a disease. The cell that is infected by the virus is called a 'host cell'. Most viruses are too

small to be seen with a normal microscope. A virus is unable to reproduce without the help

## of the host cell.

There are a few viral fish diseases, but till today researchers have not yet isolated any virus in African catfish which causes serious damage. In general, viral fish diseases are not

treatable and can cause a high mortality especially among young fish. Fish which survive

the disease, become carriers and infect other fish which have not yet been exposed.

New fish viruses are still being diagnosed, which is a serious threat to aquaculture in general. Movements of fish from one continent to the other, make it even easier for viruses to spread

over the world very quickly. One of the latest examples is the Tilapia lake virus (TilV) which

was reported in 2014 in Ecuador and Israel. At present it has been reported in Asia and also in Lake Victoria among wild populations. It was suspected of causing the mass mortality at some of the cage farms in Volta Lake, but that did not appear to be the case.

In some farms the cost of diesel exceeds the cost of labour! So, it's obvious that the diesel

consumption has to be checked and controlled by the management. Diesel is widely used

and, once stolen, easy to trade and convert into money.

Depending on the size of the generator and farm, you should have a calibrated tank, so that you can dip on a daily basis to find out how much diesel is in the tank and how much

has been consumed by the various machines per day. If the generator is small, you can

work with 50-1 jerry cans. The moment you fuel the generator tank, you note the volume and check its running hours. What you want to find out is the consumption per hour of the generator. If you know this, you can be aware of changes in the consumption of the generator per hour, and you are also able to predict the consumption for a period of time.

Always refill the generator till the brim. Running a generator on a low level of diesel in the

tank, is not good for the engine and you also lose control over the consumption of diesel

per hour.

When you combine a generator with electricity from the PHCN, you should have an alarm

system for when the PHCN-electricity goes off or comes back, in order not to overuse the

## generator or have unnoticed power outages.

Some farmers keep very low stocks of diesel or petrol and fail to have constant power for pumping water or for running their hatchery; don't be surprised that you don't get

good results at your farm. Minimising the cost of energy is actually penny wise and pound

## foolish!

Servicing the generator, either a petrol or diesel one, is of utmost importance if you don't want any nasty surprises; make a servicing programme and follow it. Servicing is different

## 13.3 CATFISH AND CHICKEN SLAUGHTER WASTE

Catfish do well on chicken slaughter waste, such as fat, blood, intestines and organs that are not eaten by humans, and aborted eggs. This waste can be put into the pond directly

or it can be processed into a moist pellet with other ingredients and then fed to the catfish.

A farm in Ibadan has been very successful in using chicken slaughter waste directly for the catfish. Their brand is known for extremely big fish! Another farm together with a slaughter house in Lagos, has been very successful with converting chicken waste into a moist pellet for catfish, and using a recirculation system to grow their catfish. They already started this

collaboration by the year 2000. These companies have their own chicken farms, slaughter

houses, freezers and machines and in both cases the slaughter waste is processed close to the fish farm. Chicken waste appears to be a very good feed for catfish. This feed can also be given in combination with ordinary pellets. However, for catfish below 500 g it is not advisable to feed raw chicken slaughter waste.

It is obvious that the water pollution at the farm will be higher with this kind of feed than with a good quality pellet, so chicken waste can only be used if enough water is available to flush the ponds.

If you are a farmer without a slaughter house and you still intend to use slaughter waste,

this could be difficult. Logistics, availability and competition for the raw materials can make it unprofitable to use these products, apart from the effort needed to keep them fresh till feeding time!

In principle the waste water of fish ponds is good for fertilising and watering crops, but it has its restrictions. First of all, the nutrients in the water are available in very low

To supply the water to the crops or the orchard is another hassle Normally, a good method is drip irrigation. during the rainy season.

## 13.5 HOW FISH EFFLUENT CAN BE USED IN NIGERIA

The fish effluent contains suspended solids, such as faeces, clusters of bacteria and dead algae, which settle rather easily. So, it is advisable to have a settling pond for all the drainage water of the farm. Let the overflow eventually return to the drainage or river,

because it will be much less contaminated. The sludge can be pumped out of the settling

tank and used to improve the amount of organic matter in the soil and increase its nutrient levels. In many parts of Nigeria, the level of organic matter in the soil is low, because it contains mostly sand. When the sludge is added to the soil, it will become more fertile.
